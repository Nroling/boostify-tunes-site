How to Get a Direct Video URL for Your Website

This guide will help you get a direct URL for your video that can be used in the Performers page.

Method 1: Upload to YouTube (Recommended)

  1. Go to YouTube and sign in
  2. Click the camera icon in the top right and select "Upload video"
  3. Follow the steps to upload your video
  4. Set the video to "Unlisted" if you don't want it to be public
  5. Once uploaded, copy the video URL from your browser
  6. Extract the video ID (the part after "v=" in the URL)
  7. Use the YouTube embed version of the Performers page
Note: YouTube embed is the most reliable way to ensure autoplay works across browsers.

Method 2: Use Cloudinary

  1. Sign up for a free Cloudinary account
  2. Go to the Media Library and click "Upload"
  3. Upload your video file
  4. Once uploaded, click on the video to view its details
  5. Look for the "Video URL" or "Secure URL"
  6. Copy this URL and use it in the direct video player version
Note: Cloudinary offers a generous free tier and optimizes videos for web delivery.

Method 3: Use a Direct Google Drive Link

  1. Upload your video to Google Drive
  2. Right-click the file and select "Get link"
  3. Make sure it's set to "Anyone with the link"
  4. Copy the link
  5. Replace the file ID in this format: https://drive.google.com/uc?export=download&id=YOUR_FILE_ID
  6. Use this URL in the direct video player version
Note: Google Drive has bandwidth limitations and may not work reliably for larger videos or high traffic.

Testing Your Video

After getting your direct URL, you can test it by:

  1. Opening a new browser tab
  2. Pasting the URL directly in the address bar
  3. If the video plays directly in the browser, it should work in your website
Important: For the best user experience, consider compressing your video to reduce file size while maintaining quality. Tools like HandBrake can help with this.
